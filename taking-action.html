Taking Action

It's important to pay attention to your own mental well being, so that you can notice when it might be time for you take action to improve your mental health.

When to take action?

There are a number of questions that you can ask yourself if you think you are experiencing lower mental well being:

  • Have you been withdrawn and not feeling like socialising?
  • Have you been isolating yourself from friends and family?
  • Have you been disappointed in your performance at work, university or any extracurricular activities you do?
  • Have you experienced a significant loss or increase in appetite?
  • Have you noticed that you are sleeping more or less than usual?
  • Have you found it more of a struggle to take care of your appearance?

Whilst this isn't an exhaustive list, it's a great place to start.

A third of students in their second year of study or above reported declining mental health, in comparison to just a quarter of first year students.
(Randstad, 2023)

What to do

When you are not feeling great, it can be hard to know what to do, or to see how taking action will help you to improve your mental health.

It's not a sprint, but a marathon, so even taking a small first step will start you on a journey to better mental well being.

39% of students have considered leaving their course because of their mental health.
(Randstad, 2023)
