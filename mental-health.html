What is Mental Health?

Introduction

Mental health is an umbrella term that covers all aspects of mental well being.
It's not only about people with mental health issues but is applicable to every human being.

The World Health Organisation (WHO) defines mental health as:

A state of mental well being that enables people to cope with the stresses of life, realise their abilities, learn well and work well, and contribute to their community.

It is an integral component of health and well being that underpins our individual and collective abilities to make decisions, build relationships and shape the world we live in.

Mental health is a basic human right
and it is crucial to personal, community and socio-economic development.

We all have mental health and can find ways to improve it, just as we can improve our physical health.

Athletes don't stop training even though they are already fit - they look for new ways to improve, and if they stop caring for their physical health, they lose their fitness.
The same can be said for mental health.

Mental Health

What makes up our mental health?

Our mental health includes our

  • emotional
  • psychological
  • and social well-being

It affects how we think, feel and act and it helps to determine

  • how we handle stress
  • relate to others
  • and make choices

Factors that affect our mental health

There are a number of factors that have the potential to impact our mental health.
Some of these are within our control, and relate to our lifestyle, including:

  • Work
  • University
  • Diet
  • Drug or alcohol consumption
  • Exercise and health
  • Sleep

However, there are also factors outside of our control that can affect our mental health, such as:

  • Financial pressure and the cost-of-living crisis
  • Poverty and living conditions
  • Genetics
  • Childhood trauma
  • Discrimination or abuse
  • Ongoing physical illness
  • Social isolation

What are the top issues impacting student mental health?

  • Academic stress 59%
  • Financial pressures 43%
  • Rising cost of living 42%
  • Health conditions 38%

Whilst not all of the things that influence our mental health state are listed above, understanding the vast range of contributors is important to understand that there are things we can do to improve our mental well being.

It's also important to note that mental health problems can happen to anybody.
